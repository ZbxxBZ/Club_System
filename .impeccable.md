## Design Context

### Users
Chinese university students aged 18-22 are the primary audience. They browse club info and sign up for events on phones and laptops, typically during breaks between classes or in the evening in dorms. Club admins and school administrators also use the system but the homepage is student-facing.

### Brand Personality
Youthful, energetic, welcoming. Three words: **vibrant, communal, effortless**. The interface should feel like stepping into a lively campus quad during club recruitment week — colorful, diverse, full of possibility.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Illustration-driven, flat/hand-drawn decorative elements with bright, warm colors. Think Feishu/Notion landing pages but with a distinctly campus/youth flavor.
- **Theme**: Light mode — students browse during the day, on bright screens, in well-lit classrooms and dorms.
- **Anti-references**: Don't look like a government admin portal. Don't look like a generic SaaS dashboard. Don't look cold or corporate.
- **Color direction**: Warm, saturated palette — coral/orange warmth balanced with teal/green freshness. Avoid the default Element Plus blue (#409EFF).

### Design Principles
1. **Gateway, not wall** — The homepage is a functional entry point. Every role should find their path within seconds.
2. **Show, don't tell** — Use illustration and visual metaphor over walls of text to communicate what the system does.
3. **Campus energy** — The design should feel alive and communal, reflecting the social nature of student clubs.
4. **Effortless navigation** — Reduce cognitive load. Clear visual hierarchy guides users to login/register/explore without thinking.
5. **Distinctive, not decorative** — Every visual element earns its place by aiding comprehension or reinforcing brand.
